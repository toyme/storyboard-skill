# Example: Short Drama Scene

## User Request

```text
Use $storyboard-skill to turn this scene into 5 storyboard image prompts:

The protagonist stands in a rainy alley holding a letter. They hesitate, tear it in half, and walk away.
```

## Expected Output Shape

```text
[SHOT ID]
scene_01_shot_001

[PURPOSE]
Establish the protagonist's isolation and the emotional weight of the letter.

[CHARACTER]
char_a_v1, adult protagonist, tired expression, damp dark coat, letter held in the right hand.

[CAMERA]
Medium-wide shot, 35mm lens, eye-level angle, slight distance from subject.

[LIGHTING]
Night rain, cool streetlamp backlight, soft reflection on wet pavement.

[EMOTION]
Quiet hesitation, shoulders slightly tense, eyes fixed on the letter.

[COMPOSITION]
Protagonist placed slightly off-center, alley lines leading toward the figure, empty negative space behind them.

[ENVIRONMENT]
Narrow urban alley, wet pavement, dim signage glow, rain visible in the light.

[CONTINUITY]
Keep the same coat, letter, rainy night setting, and cool blue-gray palette across all shots.

[NEGATIVE CONSTRAINTS]
Do not change the character's coat, add new people, make the scene sunny, or turn the letter into another object.

[IMAGE PROMPT]
Create a medium-wide cinematic image of an adult protagonist standing alone in a rainy urban alley at night, holding a paper letter in the right hand, wearing a damp dark coat, tense and exhausted. Use a 35mm eye-level frame, cool streetlamp backlight, wet pavement reflections, blue-gray color palette, quiet dramatic mood, realistic film still style, vertical 9:16 composition. Do not add extra people, change the coat, remove the letter, or make the scene bright.
```

## Notes

Continue the sequence by changing one story beat per shot: closer look at the letter, hesitation, tearing the letter, final turn away.
